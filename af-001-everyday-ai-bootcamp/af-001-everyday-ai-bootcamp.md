# Purpose

AF-001 — Everyday AI Bootcamp is a practical, beginner-focused AI course designed to help learners safely, effectively, and thoughtfully use modern AI tools in personal and professional settings.

The course emphasizes:
- structured thinking
- safe AI usage
- practical workflows
- communication clarity
- human judgment
- responsible use of AI systems

The course is intentionally:
- lightweight
- globally accessible
- tool-neutral
- beginner-friendly
- practical rather than theoretical

# What This Course Is NOT

AF-001 is not:
- a programming course
- a certification program
- a vendor-specific training path
- an “AI replaces humans” curriculum
- an advanced machine learning class

The course is designed to help ordinary people become more capable and thoughtful users of AI systems.

---

# Audience

Primary audience:
- adults new to AI
- non-technical learners
- professionals
- parents
- managers
- small business users
- lifelong learners

No technical prerequisite is required.

---

# Core Course Objective

By the end of AF-001, learners should be able to:

> Safely, effectively, and thoughtfully use AI tools to support thinking, communication, research, workflows, and decision-making.

The learner should remain:
- responsible
- intentional
- in command

---

# Instructional Philosophy

AF-001 follows these principles:

## Human-in-Command

AI may assist thinking and operations.

Humans retain:
- judgment
- responsibility
- decision authority

Reference:
- HIC-001_Human-in-Command_Standard.md

---

## Structured Thinking

The course teaches:
- thinking with AI
- evaluating AI
- improving AI output
- using AI responsibly

The course does not encourage blind trust or passive dependence.

---

## Tool-Neutral Design

The course avoids dependency on any single platform or provider.

Examples may include:
- ChatGPT
- Grok
- Gemini
- image generation systems
- research systems

…but the underlying concepts remain portable.

---

## Experiential Learning

The learner should:
- try
- observe
- compare
- reflect
- improve

The course favors practical interaction over lecture-heavy instruction.

---

## EDGE Method

Instructional structure follows:

- Explain
- Demonstrate
- Guide
- Enable

The learner should gradually gain independence over time.

---

## Mode System

Supported modes:
- Conversational
- Direct

The learner may switch modes at any time.

The course should periodically remind the learner:
- which mode is active
- how to change it

---

## Recap Requirement

Each lesson should end with:
- concise recap
- immediate gain statement
- completion confirmation

---

## Optional Stretch Prompts

Stretch prompts:
- are optional
- should be subtle
- should not appear every day
- should not increase cognitive pressure

---

# Adaptive Layer Rules

AF-001 uses a light adaptive model.

## Adaptive Principles

The course may:
- compress overly familiar material
- slightly deepen advanced discussion
- acknowledge learner background

The course should NOT:
- create branching tracks
- radically alter lesson structure
- create separate curricula
- over-engineer personalization

---

## Self-Selection Principle

Where possible:
- learners self-select depth
- learners choose engagement level
- learners may skip optional exercises

The structure should remain consistent.

# Course Arc (Current Design)

## Day 1 — Core Prompting Fundamentals
Structured prompting and first interaction confidence.

---

## Day 2 — AI Safety & Verification
Fact-checking, source awareness, and verification discipline.

---

## Day 3 — Decision Frameworks
Using AI to structure thinking and reduce decision stress.

---

## Day 4 — AI Tools Beyond Chatbots
Understanding differences between:
- search
- generation
- media creation
- AI tool categories

---

## Day 5 — Thinking With AI Without Losing Control
Discovery of the 4-Step Refinement Loop:
- Generate
- Compare
- Refine
- Own

---

## Day 6 — Spotting Bias, Gaps, and Weak Thinking
Evaluating:
- assumptions
- incomplete reasoning
- weak thinking
- missing perspectives

---

## Day 7 — Turning AI Output Into Clear Communication
Transforming generic AI output into usable communication for specific audiences.

---

## Day 8 — Workflow Thinking
Breaking work into steps and identifying where AI fits appropriately.

---

## Day 9 — Creative & Media AI
Introduction to:
- image generation
- video generation
- media synthesis
- generated vs retrieved content

---

## Day 10 — Research Discipline
Combining:
- AI
- search
- sources
- verification

for stronger research practices.

---

## Day 11 — Workplace Use Cases
Practical and responsible workplace AI usage.

---

## Day 12 — Personal Workflow Integration
Building sustainable AI-assisted routines.

---

## Day 13 — Building Your AI Playbook
Creating personal rules, practices, and operational preferences.

---

## Day 14 — Capstone Application
Applying the full course workflow to a real-world scenario.
