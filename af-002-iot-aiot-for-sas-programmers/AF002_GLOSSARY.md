## AI-Assisted Observation
The use of AI systems to assist humans in identifying, labeling, or interpreting operational activity from telemetry or visual input.

## AIoT
Artificial Intelligence of Things. The combination of IoT telemetry systems with AI-assisted interpretation, analysis, or automation.

## Anomaly
An unexpected or unusual operational condition that may require investigation.

## Annotated Image
An image containing visual overlays such as labels, bounding boxes, or confidence values.

## Architecture Follows Data
The principle that operational architecture decisions should be driven by the needs of the telemetry, workflow, and operational environment.

## Active Evidence
The simultaneous presentation of visual evidence, telemetry data, and operational questions during investigation.

## Bounded Uncertainty
Recognition that telemetry and operational evidence may be incomplete, delayed, or imperfect.

## Bounding Box
A rectangular coordinate region used to represent the detected position of an object within an image or frame.

Bounding boxes are commonly represented using:
- x1
- y1
- x2
- y2

Bounding boxes transform visual detections into structured telemetry suitable for operational investigation using SAS.

## Computer Vision
Technology allowing systems to interpret images or video frames for operational analysis.

## Confidence Score
A numeric estimate representing how confident an AI model is in a detection or classification.

## Confidence Threshold
A configured minimum confidence value required before accepting a detection.

## Congestion
An operational condition where movement, workflow, or throughput becomes delayed or restricted.

## Coordinate System
A structured positional system used to identify locations within an image or operational environment.

## CSV Import
The process of loading comma-separated telemetry data into SAS for analysis.

## Dashboard Abstraction
The loss of operational detail caused when dashboards summarize or simplify telemetry.

## DATA Step
A SAS programming structure used to create, modify, or process datasets.

## DataFrame
A structured tabular data structure commonly used in pandas.

## Dependency
A required software package or component needed for a script or workflow to operate correctly.

## Detection Confidence
The reported confidence level associated with a detection event.

## Detection Event
A telemetry event representing the detection of an object or condition.

## Detection Label
The descriptive category assigned to a detected object.

## Detection Model
An AI model used to identify or classify objects within telemetry or images.

## Deterministic URL
A stable predictable URL structure designed to improve survivability and restartability.

## Event Stream
A sequence of operational telemetry events occurring over time.

## Evidence vs Truth
The principle that telemetry provides evidence requiring human interpretation rather than unquestionable truth.

## False Negative
A missed detection where an object or condition exists but is not identified.

## False Positive
An incorrect detection where an object or condition is reported inaccurately.

## Human-in-Command
A system design philosophy where humans remain responsible for operational interpretation and final decision-making.

## Human Oversight
Human review or supervision of operational telemetry, automation, or AI-assisted systems.

## Human Review
The process of a human evaluating telemetry, detections, or operational evidence.

## IoT
Internet of Things. Operational systems that generate telemetry from connected devices, sensors, or systems.

## Known-Good Output
An example output known to represent correct workflow execution.

## Object Detection
AI-assisted identification of objects or entities within images or video frames.

## Occupancy
The presence or concentration of objects or people within a defined operational region.

## OpenCV
An open-source computer vision library commonly used for image and video processing.

## Operational Ambiguity
Operational uncertainty caused by incomplete, delayed, conflicting, or unclear telemetry.

## Operational Investigation
The process of interpreting telemetry and operational evidence to understand real-world conditions.

## Operational Judgment
Human reasoning used to interpret telemetry and determine operational significance.

## Operational Telemetry
Telemetry representing operational activity, workflows, events, or environmental conditions.

## Operational Visibility
The degree to which telemetry allows understanding of operational conditions.

## Operational Workflow
A repeatable operational process or sequence of activities.

## pandas
A Python data analysis library commonly used for tabular telemetry processing.

## pip
Python’s package installation utility.

## PowerShell
A Windows command-line and scripting environment used throughout AF-002 setup and workflow examples.

## PROC FREQ
A SAS procedure used to summarize and count telemetry events or categories.

## PROC MEANS
A SAS procedure used to calculate descriptive statistics for telemetry data.

## PROC SGPLOT
A SAS procedure used to create operational charts and visualizations.

## PROC TIMEPLOT
A SAS procedure used to visualize telemetry or operational activity across time.

## Python
A programming language used in AF-002 for telemetry generation and operational workflow support.

## Python Virtual Environment
An isolated Python environment used to manage project-specific dependencies.

## Recovery Path
A documented method allowing learners to recover from workflow or setup problems.

## Region Analysis
Operational investigation using predefined spatial zones such as:
- restricted
- loading
- pedestrian
- staging

## Region Transition
Movement of an object or entity from one operational region into another.

## Restricted Zone
A defined operational area where access or activity may be limited or monitored.

## Restartability
The ability to safely restart a workflow, lesson, or operational process.

## Runtime Environment
The active software environment where scripts and workflows execute.

## SAS Operational Analysis
The use of SAS procedures and workflows to interpret telemetry and operational conditions.

## Script Execution
The process of running a script or command-based workflow.

## Semantic Telemetry
Telemetry representing meaningful labeled operational events rather than raw signals alone.

## Small Understandable Operational Systems
An AF-002 design principle emphasizing operational systems that learners can reasonably understand, investigate, and explain.

## Spatial Telemetry
Structured telemetry representing the position, movement, occupancy, or region interaction of objects within a visual environment.

## Structured Data
Data organized into rows and columns suitable for analysis.

## Survivability
The ability of a workflow, lesson, or operational process to continue functioning under real-world conditions.

## Telemetry
Structured operational data representing events, activity, states, or measurements.

## Telemetry Drift
Changes in telemetry behavior, accuracy, timing, or interpretation over time.

## Telemetry Freshness
The timeliness and recency of telemetry relative to operational conditions.

## Timestamp Lineage
Understanding what a timestamp represents, such as event time, transmission time, or processing time.

## Ultralytics
The organization and software ecosystem associated with YOLO object detection models used in AF-002 examples.

## venv
The standard Python virtual environment module.

## Webcam Telemetry
Telemetry generated from webcam-based operational observation workflows.

## Workflow Congestion
Operational slowdown caused by excessive traffic, occupancy, or process bottlenecks.

## YOLO
“You Only Look Once,” a family of object-detection models used for real-time image analysis.
