# Core Principle

```text
Do not re-invent the wheel.
```

This phrase was repeatedly taught to large financial-services sales teams by sales professional:

```text
Shane Rudman
```

during operational sales training.  The purpose was to encourage salespeople to use what is known to work and be most effective in the sales process isntead of trying to search for a better way.

The principle now appears highly applicable to:

* instructional design
* operational engineering
* governance work
* telemetry systems
* AI-assisted development
* curriculum stabilization
* survivability engineering

and AF-series development generally.

---

# Operational Meaning

The principle does NOT mean:

* never innovate
* never redesign
* never improve
* never rethink architecture

The principle DOES mean:

```text
Before inventing a new solution,
review:
- prior successes
- prior failures
- prior patterns
- prior lessons learned
```

This became critically important during:

* AF-002 Lesson 04 stabilization
* pacing recovery
* startup redesign
* runtime survivability work
