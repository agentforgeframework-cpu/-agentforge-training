# AF002 Instructional Identity and Pedagogy — Lessons Learned

- Operational realism beats cinematic escalation.
- Small understandable operational systems scale instructional trust better than hyperscale fantasy.
- Human-in-Command works best when embedded operationally, not lectured philosophically.
- Telemetry ambiguity is instructional fuel when bounded carefully.
- Operational confidence must be earned gradually before meeting-style capstones work.
- Architecture discussions work best after learners gain operational confidence through evidence.
- SAS programmers already understand telemetry thinking more than they initially realize.
- SAS remained strongest when positioned as operational reasoning tooling rather than reporting software.
- Warehouse telemetry proved understandable enough for broad learners while remaining operationally believable.
