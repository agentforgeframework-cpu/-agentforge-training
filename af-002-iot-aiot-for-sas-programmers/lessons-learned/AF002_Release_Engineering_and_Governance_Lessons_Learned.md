# AF002 Release Engineering and Governance — Lessons Learned

- Deterministic raw GitHub URLs substantially improved clean-room survivability.
- Freeze discipline prevented architectural entropy late in development.
- PowerShell-based release engineering proved more reliable than repeated AI-only bulk edits.
- Validation tooling itself requires survivability hardening and governance.
- Release engineering emerged naturally as the course matured.
- Governance became operationally necessary once instructional architecture stabilized.
- AF-002 increasingly behaves like a deployable operational training platform rather than a traditional course.
