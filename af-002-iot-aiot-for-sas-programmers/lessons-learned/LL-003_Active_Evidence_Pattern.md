# LL-003 — ACTIVE EVIDENCE Instructional Pattern

Status:
Accepted

Project:
AF-002 — IoT/AIoT for SAS Programmers

Date:

---

# Observation

Learners reasoned more effectively when:
- the operational image
- telemetry row
- operational question

remained simultaneously visible.

This pattern became known as:

```text
ACTIVE EVIDENCE
```

---

# Runtime Discovery

Learners naturally:
- referenced the image repeatedly
- re-examined telemetry rows
- questioned evidence quality
- compared visual evidence to telemetry

This behavior strengthened:
- operational skepticism
- evidence interpretation
- Human-in-Command reasoning

---

# Failure Pattern Before ACTIVE EVIDENCE

Before ACTIVE EVIDENCE:
- learners lost operational grounding
- telemetry became abstract
- discussions drifted into theory
- operational context weakened

---

# Instructional Improvement

Keeping evidence continuously visible improved:
- pacing
- investigation continuity
- operational reasoning
- telemetry interpretation

The lesson increasingly felt like:
```text
operational investigation
```

instead of:
```text
abstract AI instruction.
```

---

# Recommended Runtime Pattern

Use:
- operational image
- telemetry row
- operational question

together during investigative discussion.

Avoid hiding or replacing operational evidence prematurely.

---

# Human-in-Command Alignment

ACTIVE EVIDENCE strongly reinforces:
- evidence-based reasoning
- telemetry validation
- operational interpretation
- skepticism before conclusion

---

# Operational Conclusion

Learners investigate telemetry more effectively when evidence remains continuously visible during operational reasoning.
